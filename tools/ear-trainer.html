How to train your ear

Listen to the two notes, then pick the interval. If you miss it, the correct answer lights up — replay it and burn the sound into memory. Reference songs help: a Perfect 4th is "Here Comes the Bride," a Perfect 5th opens "Twinkle Twinkle," an Octave is "Somewhere Over the Rainbow."

Why it matters

  • Play by ear instead of relying only on sheet music.
  • Transcribe songs you love faster.
  • Improvise and harmonize with confidence.
