How to actually practice music as an adult

Adults don't lack motivation — they lack systems. Between work and life, "I'll practice when I feel like it" loses every time. Here's a framework that survives a real schedule.

1. Shrink the session, not the ambition

Twenty focused minutes beats a vague "hour" you keep postponing. Block a fixed, small window and protect it. Consistency compounds far faster than occasional marathons.

2. Practice the hard part, not the whole part

Playing a piece top to bottom mostly rehearses what you already know. Isolate the two or three bars that break down and drill those slowly until they're clean, then reconnect them.

3. Slow is fast

Use a metronome below your comfort tempo. Accuracy first; speed is a by-product of clean repetition. Raise the tempo 2–4 BPM only once a passage is reliable.

4. Calibrate difficulty to your level

Material that's too hard kills momentum; too easy wastes time. The sweet spot is "just beyond comfortable." This is exactly what structured platforms automate — matching piece difficulty and length to your current proficiency.

5. Track it

What gets measured gets done. Even a simple log of what you practised and at what tempo turns fuzzy effort into visible progress.
